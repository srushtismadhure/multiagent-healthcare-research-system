# Reimbursement and Payment Models — Research Workflow SOP

**Bucket:** Reimbursement and Payment Models  
**Agent:** `agents/reimbursement_agent.md`  
**Run frequency:** Weekly (or on-demand)

---

## Objective

Track CMS payment model experiments, reimbursement for AI-assisted care, value-based care contract shifts, and gaps affecting women's health and chronic pain.

---

1. `CMS CMMI payment model 2025 update`

## Structured Output Contract

The workflow should produce structured findings that can support:

- Summary
- Key Findings
- What This Means
- Limitations

Use a backing markdown table or equivalent structured rows with these fields:

| Payment Model | What Changed | Who It Affects | Women's Health / Chronic Pain Relevance | Urgency / Time Sensitivity | Confidence | Source Quality Tier | Recommended Action | Source Link |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------|----------------------------|------------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|

Field notes:
- **Urgency / Time Sensitivity** is heuristic and should reflect effective dates, payment deadlines, or near-term rule changes.
- **Confidence** should reflect source strength and extraction depth.
- **Source Quality Tier** should map to `memory/source_quality.md`.
- **Recommended Action** may be completed by the Orchestrator when the evidence is real but shallow.

---

## Focus Areas

1. Medicare Advantage rate and prior-authorization changes
2. CMMI experiments being expanded, sunsetted, or reevaluated
3. AI reimbursement and code coverage signals
4. Chronic pain reimbursement gaps
5. Women's health coverage and access gaps

---

## Edge Cases

- Always date-stamp CMMI and reimbursement findings
- Specify the actual payment model type when possible
- Track CMS rules and congressional activity separately for prior authorization
- If there are no major new announcements, summarize the status of the most material active models
